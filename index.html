Geoflow: Geometry-Conditioned Flow Matching via Canonical Point Clouds for SE(3)-Consistent Robotic Imitation Learning

Paper Code

Overview of our work

Abstract

Vision-based imitation learning using 3D point clouds has emerged as a critical paradigm for robots to acquire complex manipulation skills, leveraging rich geometric priors and view-invariant spatial representations. However, existing point cloud-based policies often suffer from instability and poor generalization when deployed in real-world environments characterized by sensor noise and varying viewpoints. To address this bottleneck, we propose Geoflow, a geometry-conditioned flow matching framework designed for SE(3)-consistent robot manipulation from point cloud observations. Geoflow constructs a temporal-aware canonical representation by embedding SE(3)-equivariance into temporal modeling, and directly drives a geometry-conditioned flow matching strategy using the synergistically aggregated cross-frame features. This concurrently strengthens perceptual robustness against unstructured disturbances and adaptively reshapes probability flow paths to reduce the traversal of low-likelihood regions, promoting high-fidelity action synthesis. Extensive evaluations across simulated benchmarks (Robomimic and RLBench) and real-world deployments demonstrate that Geoflow consistently outperforms state-of-the-art baselines in both success rates and robustness. Particularly in constrained tasks, our method maintains robust performance, outperforming existing flow-based and equivariant baselines by an average of 18.8%. The codes and more details are available at https://geoflow-policy.github.io/.

Figure presents the complete GeoFlow framework, which synergizes robust perception with precise motion generation. The pipeline begins with a Spatio-Temporal Canonical Representation module (left) that processes multi-step point clouds into a standardized coordinate system, ensuring SE(3)-equivariant feature extraction that is resilient to viewpoint shifts. These stable geometric features are then fed into the Curvature-Guided Flow Matching Action Head (right), which generates high-fidelity action trajectories by learning probability-aware paths rather than simple linear interpolations.

(a) illustrates the Data Canonicalization Pipeline, the core mechanism that decouples intrinsic object geometry from global pose variations. To achieve this, the system first decentralizes the raw point cloud to remove translation bias , then utilizes an SO(3)-equivariant network to estimate the relative rotation , and finally applies an inverse transformation to map the observation into a unified "canonical" space. This explicit geometric standardization ensures that the policy learns consistent manipulation logic regardless of how the camera or objects are oriented in the scene. (b) visualizes the theoretical advantage of our Curvature-Guided Flow Matching approach compared to traditional methods. While standard Rectified Flow forces the generation process along a rigid linear path (grey dashed line) that may cross into physically implausible low-likelihood regions, GeoFlow introduces learnable curvature coefficients to define a flexible non-linear trajectory (blue solid line). This allows the model to adaptively align the probability transport path with the natural geometry of the action manifold, resulting in smoother and more stable motion generation.

Figure demonstrates the real-world performance of GeoFlow across four diverse manipulation tasks: Sorting parts, Tower of Hanoi, Battery insertion, and Hammer cleanup. The execution sequences highlight the robot's capability to handle contact-rich operations and precise pick-and-place actions, such as the tight-tolerance alignment required for inserting a battery. These results validate that the proposed geometric constraints and curvature guidance successfully enable zero-shot transfer from simulation to real-world platforms without requiring additional fine-tuning.
